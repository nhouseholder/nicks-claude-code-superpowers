# Audience Psychology Breakdown

Turn audience understanding into specific messaging angles and content topics that stop the scroll and build trust.

## When This Fires

- "Break down my target audience"
- "Who am I talking to?"
- "What messaging will resonate?"
- "Help me understand my audience"
- Before creating content pillars or calendars (run this first)

## Prerequisites

1. **Read `brand-context.md`** in the project root. Required — do not proceed without it.
2. **Check for existing `audience-profile.md`** — update rather than replace if it exists.

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Demographic Profile

Build the surface-level picture:

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Age range** | [From brand context + inference] |
| **Gender split** | [If relevant to the niche] |
| **Income level** | [Affects pricing sensitivity and content tone] |
| **Tech savviness** | [Determines jargon level] |
| **Job titles** | [Common roles in this audience] |
| **Geographic focus** | [Where they are] |

### Phase 2: Psychographic Deep Dive

This is where the real value lives. For each dimension, provide 3-5 specific examples:

**Frustrations (What keeps them up at night)**
- [Specific frustration] → Messaging angle: "[How to frame content around this]"

**Desires (What they dream about achieving)**
- [Specific desire] → Messaging angle: "[How to frame content around this]"

**Fears (What stops them from acting)**
- [Specific fear] → Messaging angle: "[How to address/overcome this]"

**Daily Habits (How they consume content)**
- Morning routine: [What platforms, what content types]
- Work breaks: [Quick-hit content they consume]
- Evening wind-down: [Longer content they engage with]

**Identity (How they see themselves)**
- "I am the kind of person who..." [3-5 identity statements]
- This matters because: people share content that reinforces their self-image

### Phase 4: Messaging Angle Matrix

Convert insights into actionable messaging:

| Angle Type | Hook Formula | Example | Why It Works |
|-----------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| **Pain agitation** | "Tired of [frustration]?" | [Specific example] | Validates their experience |
| **Aspiration** | "What if you could [desire]?" | [Specific example] | Shows the possibility |
| **Fear reversal** | "[Fear] is a myth. Here's why." | [Specific example] | Removes barriers |
| **Identity** | "Smart [role] already know..." | [Specific example] | Tribal belonging |
| **Proof** | "[Result] in [timeframe]" | [Specific example] | Removes skepticism |
| **Contrarian** | "Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong." | [Specific example] | Pattern interrupt |

### Phase 5: Content Topic Generator

From the angles above, generate 20 specific content topics:
- 5 that address top frustrations
- 5 that paint the desired outcome
- 5 that overcome fears/objections
- 5 that reinforce audience identity

Each topic includes: headline, hook sentence, key message, CTA.

### Phase 6: ICP Builder (Jobs-to-be-Done Framework)

Build a one-page Ideal Customer Profile using JTBD:

**Functional Job** — The specific problem they solve by consuming content like yours:
- "When I [situation], I want to [action], so I can [outcome]."

**Emotional Job** — How they want to FEEL after seeing your posts:
- [3-5 emotional states they're seeking]

**Social Job** — How they want OTHERS to see them for following you:
- "I follow [brand] because it makes me look [perception]"

**Their Exact Language** — How THEY describe their problem (their words, not yours):
- Research from communities, reviews, forums, Reddit
- [5-10 actual phrases/complaints in their vocabulary]

**Scroll-Stopping Topics** — 3 content topics derived from JTBD that stop their scroll:
1. [Topic] — maps to [which job]
2. [Topic] — maps to [which job]
3. [Topic] — maps to [which job]

**Belief Correction** — 1 common belief they hold that you can publicly correct to build instant authority:
- "Most people think [wrong belief]. The truth is [correct frame]."
- Why correcting this builds authority: [explanation]

Format the ICP as a one-page reference card saved to `icp.md` — something to reference before writing every post.

## Output

Save to `audience-profile.md` in the project root (ICP goes to `icp.md`). Structure:
1. Audience snapshot (1 paragraph)
2. Demographic table
3. Psychographic deep dive
4. Content consumption patterns
5. Messaging angle matrix
6. 20 ready-to-use content topics

## Rules

1. **Specific over generic** — "data-driven sports bettors aged 25-40" not "people interested in sports"
2. **Evidence-based** — derive from brand context, not assumptions. Flag when you're inferring.
3. **Actionable angles** — every insight must map to a messaging approach
4. **Anti-slop** — no "in today's digital age" filler. Real psychology, real angles.
5. **Cross-reference** — if `social-strategy.md` exists, align audience work with it
