# Authority Positioning Plan

Build a positioning strategy that separates you from everyone else in your space and makes you the go-to name.

## When This Fires

- "Build my positioning strategy"
- "How do I stand out in my niche?"
- "Create my personal brand strategy"
- "Authority positioning plan"
- When brand-context.md exists but positioning is unclear

## Prerequisites

1. **Read `brand-context.md`** — Required.
2. **Read `audience-profile.md`** if it exists — positioning must resonate with the target audience.
3. **Read `social-strategy.md`** if it exists — positioning must align with overall strategy.

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Competitive Landscape Scan

Map the current players:

Web research recommended: search for top voices in the niche, analyze their content, identify white space.

### Phase 2: Unique Mechanism

Define what makes your approach fundamentally different:

- **The Problem With Everyone Else:** [What the standard approach gets wrong]
- **Your Unique Mechanism:** [Your proprietary method/framework/approach]
- **Why It Works:** [The logic behind your approach — make it intuitive]
- **Name It:** [Give your method a memorable name if it doesn't have one]

Example: "Most sports bettors rely on gut feel. OctagonAI uses machine learning trained on 500+ fights to find edges the market misses."

### Phase 3: Proof Stack

Authority requires proof. Build your evidence hierarchy:

| Proof Type | Your Evidence | Content Format |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Results** | [Specific outcomes you've achieved] | Case studies, screenshots |
| **Process** | [Show how you work] | Behind-the-scenes, build-in-public |
| **Knowledge** | [Deep expertise demonstrations] | Breakdowns, analyses, tutorials |
| **Social** | [Others vouching for you] | Testimonials, mentions, collaborations |
| **Consistency** | [Track record over time] | Regular content, streak metrics |

### Phase 4: Positioning Statement

Craft the core positioning:

```
FOR: [specific audience]
WHO: [have this problem/desire]
[BRAND] IS THE: [category descriptor]
THAT: [unique value proposition]
UNLIKE: [alternatives]
BECAUSE: [proof/reason to believe]
```

Then distill to:
- **Twitter bio version** (160 chars)
- **Instagram bio version** (150 chars)
- **Elevator pitch** (30 seconds)
- **One-liner** (10 words or less)

### Phase 5: Authority Content Framework

Map content types that build authority:

| Content Type | Purpose | Frequency | Example |
|-------------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Hot takes** | Show you think differently | 2-3x/week | "[Contrarian opinion on industry trend]" |
| **Deep dives** | Demonstrate mastery | 1x/week | "Complete breakdown of [complex topic]" |
| **Results posts** | Prove it works | 1-2x/week | "This week: [specific results]" |
| **Process reveals** | Build trust through transparency | 1x/week | "Here's exactly how I [process]" |
| **Predictions** | Show forward thinking | Monthly | "What I expect to happen with [trend]" |

### Phase 6: Authority Milestones

Define what "authority" looks like at each stage:

| Stage | Metric | Timeline | Actions |
|-------|--------|----------|---------|
| **Unknown** | <500 followers | Month 1-2 | Daily posting, engage in communities |
| **Emerging** | 500-2K followers | Month 3-6 | Guest content, collaborations |
| **Recognized** | 2K-10K followers | Month 6-12 | Speaking, interviews, features |
| **Authority** | 10K+ followers | Year 1-2 | Products, premium content, media |

## Output

Save to `authority-positioning.md` in the project root. Structure:
1. Positioning statement (all versions)
2. Unique mechanism definition
3. Competitive landscape map
4. Proof stack inventory
5. Authority content framework
6. Growth milestones with actions

## Rules

1. **Differentiation is mandatory** — if your positioning could apply to any competitor, it's not positioning
2. **Proof over claims** — every authority claim needs evidence
3. **Niche down** — better to own a small category than compete in a large one
4. **Anti-slop** — no "thought leader" or "influencer" language. Specific, concrete, real.
5. **Buildable** — the plan must be executable by one person starting today
